About

What's with all the dragons?

My name, Vahag, comes from Vahagn (Վահագն) — the ancient Armenian god of fire, thunder, and war, known as the Vishapakagh, or "Dragon Reaper." In Armenian mythology, Vahagn was born from fire and fought the vishaps — primordial dragons of chaos — to protect his people. He remains one of Armenia's most enduring mythological figures, with statues across Yerevan honoring his legacy.

I was also born in the Year of the Dragon. Between the namesake and the birth year, the dragon motif felt less like a choice and more like an inheritance — so I'm building a brand around it. From project names like Dragonote and Tomato Dragon to the visual identity across my work, the dragon thread runs through everything.

What is this site built with?

Pure HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks, no build tools, no dependencies. It runs entirely as a static site on GitHub Pages. The design philosophy was intentional: clean, performant, well-crafted software doesn't need a mountain of tooling behind it.
